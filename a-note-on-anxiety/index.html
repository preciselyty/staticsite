A note on Anxiety

I’m worried. And yes, I do have specific problems that I am worried about. And I can say, “oh, I’m really anxious, and this problem is worrying me.” The thing is though, is that those two things aren’t completely linked. Because really, I’m going to continue to worry even after that problem is solved. Always, it seems like. Not 100% of the time, of course. There are times when I'm not worried. When my mind is occupied, my anxiety seems to fade a bit. But finishing one task or solving one problem doesn’t make that feeling of anxiety go away. Not for very long. Because soon, I'll move on to a new thing to worry about. Perhaps something that hadn't even crossed my mind before. Or maybe I won't be able to think of anything specific to worry about at some point. What happens then? Well, I worry because I have nothing to worry about.

Keep in mind that I use the word worry. I’m not just constantly thinking about the next problem that could come up. Thinking ahead is great. Planning is great. But I’m not thinking and planning ahead. Instead, I’m just worried. Just a feeling of anxiety. Rather than thinking, I would use the word wondering. I wonder how this could go wrong. I wonder if everything is going to be okay. I wonder how everything is going to be okay, and I wonder why I feel like I need to be doing something right then.

And then it always is okay. You know, everything. So you might assume that I'd realize at some point that I don't need to listen to my anxiety. All of these tiny little things that I insist on stressing myself out over have never caused the end of the world. And I'm working on that. But it always feels like things just aren’t going to be okay when I settle on my next thing to be worried about. So I worry some more...
